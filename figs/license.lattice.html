Understanding the Lattice

Direction: Arrows point from less restrictive to more restrictive licenses. A license higher in the lattice is more restrictive than licenses below it.

Compatibility: Code under a less restrictive license can generally be incorporated into projects with more restrictive licenses, but not vice versa.

Key Insight: The lattice shows that permissive licenses (MIT, BSD) are at the bottom because they impose fewer restrictions, while copyleft licenses (GPL, AGPL) are higher because they require derivative works to maintain the same freedoms.
